Product Requirements Document

Job Architecture Wizard

This document outlines the functionality and technical requirements for a web-based tool designed to guide users through defining and managing an organization's roles, skills, and career paths.

1. Introduction

This document outlines the functionality and technical requirements for the **Job Architecture Wizard**, a web-based tool designed to guide users through defining, organizing, and managing an organization's roles, skills, and career paths. This wizard provides a structured, step-by-step approach to building a comprehensive job architecture, with features for data entry, editing, and visualization.

2. Goals

  • Streamline Job Architecture Definition: Provide a user-friendly interface to define organizational structures (organizations, business units, departments), job groupings (job families, role groups), and job levels.
  • Centralize Skill Management: Integrate skill definitions and proficiency levels into job profiles.
  • Facilitate Career Pathing: Enable the creation and visualization of potential career progression paths based on defined job levels and skills.
  • Support Data Management: Allow users to add, edit, and delete architectural components.
  • Provide AI-Powered Insights (Future/Planned): Incorporate AI capabilities for suggestions, gap analysis, and review processes.

3. Scope

The current scope covers the front-end functionality of the Job Architecture Wizard, including the multi-step navigation, data input/display for various architectural components, and client-side data management using mock data. Backend integration for persistent storage and actual AI processing is outside the immediate scope but will be considered for future phases.

4. User Stories

As an HR Administrator, I want to define my company's organizational structure (Business Units, Departments) so that I can accurately categorize jobs.

As an HR Administrator, I want to create and manage Job Families and Role Groups to organize similar jobs.

As an HR Administrator, I want to define Job Levels with associated responsibilities, salary ranges, and skill levels so that I can establish clear career progression.

As an HR Administrator, I want to view and edit existing organizational data easily to keep the job architecture up-to-date.

As an HR Administrator, I want to see potential career paths for a given job level to help employees plan their development.

As an HR Administrator, I want to search and filter job levels by various criteria (Business Unit, Department, Role Group) to quickly find relevant information.

As an HR Administrator, I want to add, edit, and delete skills associated with job profiles.

As an HR Administrator, I want to review AI-generated suggestions for job profiles, skill gaps, and career paths.

5. Functional Requirements

5.1. Stepper Navigation

  • FR1.1: The wizard shall display a clear, horizontal stepper navigation at the top, indicating the current step, completed steps, and upcoming steps.
  • FR1.2: Each step in the stepper shall be clickable, allowing users to navigate directly to any previously visited or future step.
  • FR1.3: Steps identified as "AI-Powered" shall have a distinct visual style (e.g., an icon, a different color). AI-Powered
  • FR1.4: "Back" and "Next Step" buttons shall be available for sequential navigation.
  • FR1.5: The "Back" button shall be disabled on the first step.
  • FR1.6: A "Finish" button shall appear on the final step, replacing the "Next Step" button, to conclude the wizard.
  • FR1.7: The stepper navigation shall be horizontally scrollable on smaller screens, with fade effects indicating more content is available.
  • FR1.8: Clicking the "Job Architecture" heading shall reset the wizard to the first step.

5.2. Data Management (CRUD Operations)

For each architectural component (Organization, Business Unit, Department, Job Family, Job Level, Job Profile, Skill Gap, Career Path):

  • FR2.1: The system shall display a table-like view of existing data for the current step.
  • FR2.2: The system shall provide an "Add" button to create new entries.
  • FR2.3: The system shall provide "Edit" functionality for existing entries, opening a modal with pre-filled forms.
  • FR2.4: The system shall provide "Delete" functionality for existing entries, with a confirmation prompt.
  • FR2.5: All data modifications (add, edit, delete) shall be reflected immediately in the UI.
  • FR2.6: Data shall be loaded from consolidated-mock-data.json on initialization and managed client-side within the appData object.

5.3. Specific Step Functionality

  • Job Levels: The Job Levels table shall support searching by text, filtering by Business Unit, Department, and Role Group, and pagination. A "Career Path" button shall be available for each Job Level.
  • Job Profiles: Users can define and manage profiles with skills and proficiency levels. AI-Powered
  • Skill Gaps: A placeholder for future skill gap analysis. It is marked as AI-Powered. AI-Powered
  • Career Paths: Shall generate and display potential career progressions. AI-Powered

5.4. Modals

  • FR4.1: A generic modal component shall be used for all add, edit, and delete operations.
  • FR4.2: The modal shall support multiple tabs for organizing form content.
  • FR4.3: The modal shall include "Cancel" and "Confirm" buttons.
  • FR4.4: The modal shall be responsive and accessible.

9. Skills Ontology & Data Model

The skills ontology is a foundational component for all talent management capabilities. It provides the structured, canonical list of skills and skill sets that is required to power features like Skill Gap Analysis, Personalized Learning Recommendations, and Career Pathing. The skills ontology team is responsible for managing this master data and ensuring its quality.

9.1. Required Data Elements

For each skill, the ontology must provide the following fields to be effective. The hierarchy is defined as **Skill Group > Skill Set > Skill**.

  • `skillId`: A unique, consistent alphanumeric identifier for the skill (e.g., `s-pyt-001`). This ID is critical for data integrity when referencing skills across different systems (HRIS, LMS).
  • `name`: The human-readable name of the skill (e.g., "Python Programming," "Active Listening"). This is what is displayed to the user.
  • `description`: A brief, clear description of the skill. This helps users and AI models understand the skill's scope and meaning.
  • `category`: A high-level classification for the skill (e.g., "Programming Languages," "Project Management," "Data Analysis"). This is used for filtering, searching, and organizing skills.
  • `proficiencyLevels`: A structured array of proficiency levels, each with a `level` and a `description` (e.g., "Beginner," "Intermediate," "Advanced"). These levels are defined for the *individual skill* and are essential for a standardized skill assessment in the Skill Gap Analysis feature.
  • `relatedSkills`: An optional array of `skillId`s that are conceptually related or prerequisites. This is a key input for the AI to make intelligent, non-linear recommendations in the Career Pathing "Radar View."
  • `Skill Set`: A field to link a skill to its parent Skill Set (e.g., the skill "Python Programming" belongs to the Skill Set "Software Development"). This enables a hierarchical view of skills.
  • `Skill Group`: A field to link a Skill Set to its parent Skill Group (e.g., the Skill Set "Software Development" belongs to the Skill Group "Engineering & Technology"). This represents the highest level of hierarchy.

9.2. Skills-as-a-Service (SaaS) Model

The skills ontology must function as a centralized service, providing a master source of truth for all skill data. This requires a robust backend that supports:

  • CRUD Operations: The ability for authorized users or systems to Create, Read, Update, and Delete skill definitions.
  • Version Control: A system to manage different versions of the ontology to handle updates without breaking historical data.
  • API Access: A well-documented API for other applications (like the Job Architecture Wizard and the Talent Marketplace) to query and integrate skill data.

10. Import/Export Functionality

The wizard shall provide a dedicated feature for bulk data management, enabling users to import a pre-formatted file to populate the job architecture or export their existing data for backup and offline editing.

  • FR10.1: The system shall provide an "Import" button to allow users to upload a CSV or similar structured data file.
  • FR10.2: An "Export" button shall generate and download a file containing the entire job architecture data currently in the system.
  • FR10.3: An "upload template" link shall be provided to give users a clear structure for their data file. The template will be available at: View Upload Template
  • FR10.4: The import process shall include validation checks to ensure the uploaded data conforms to the required structure and schema.

10.5. Comprehensive Role Import Schema

This schema includes all necessary fields for a robust role architecture that powers advanced features like dynamic career paths, skill gap analysis, personalized learning, and connections.

1. Core Role Definition & Hierarchy

  • `Role Title`: The official name of the role.
  • `Role Summary`: A brief description used for internal directories and search.
  • `Job Description`: The full-text, detailed definition of the role and its duties.
  • `Business Unit / Division`: High-level organizational group.
  • `Department`: Specific functional area.
  • `Job Family`: A grouping of related roles for career pathing.
  • `Role Group / Team`: The immediate team for operational context.
  • `Role Code / ID`: A unique, consistent identifier for the role.

2. Skills, Competencies, & Expertise

These fields are crucial for mapping skills and behavioral traits to a role. The two tables are distinct but related, allowing for granular skill mapping while also defining broader behavioral expectations. The data would be structured with a unique ID for each skill or competency, and a proficiency level where applicable.

  • `Skills Table`: An array or structured list of hard skills (e.g., `[{"id": "s-pyt-001", "level": "Advanced"}, {"id": "s-sql-003", "level": "Intermediate"}]`).
  • `Soft Skills Table`: This is a structured array, similar to the `Skills Table`, but for soft skills. It would contain a list of skill IDs from the master skills ontology and their associated proficiency levels. This is for granular mapping of specific soft skills like "Active Listening" or "Time Management." (e.g., `[{"id": "ss-act-001", "level": "Intermediate"}, {"id": "ss-tm-002", "level": "Proficient"}]`).
  • `Behavioral Competencies`: This is a list of key behavioral competencies expected in the role, linking back to your master ontology. These are often broader, more abstract concepts that may be composed of multiple soft skills. For example, the "Communication" competency could be supported by the soft skills "Active Listening" and "Public Speaking." (e.g., `["Leadership", "Collaboration", "Customer Focus"]`).
  • `Tools & Technologies`: Specific software or tools required for the role.

3. Career Pathing & Relationships

  • `Level`: The seniority level of the role (e.g., IC3, M1).
  • `Feeder Roles`: A list of role IDs that are common entry points into this role.
  • `Reports To`: The Role Code of the direct management position.
  • `Reporting Lines`: Defines the formal management structure.
  • `Matrix Relationships`: An array of role IDs to which this role has a dotted-line or project-based reporting relationship.
  • `Career Track`: The official path a role belongs to (e.g., "Management," "Individual Contributor").
  • `Career Path â€“ Next Roles`: A structured list of suggested next roles.
  • `Career Path Mapping`: A structured field that defines the relationships between roles to build the "Subway Map View."
  • `AI Recommendations`: A placeholder for a structured field that can be populated by the AI to define custom paths.

4. Employee Development & Learning

  • `Education Requirements`: Required degrees or certifications.
  • `Certifications`: Specific certifications needed.
  • `Learning Resources`: An array of URLs or IDs linking to recommended courses, articles, or videos from the Learning Management System.

5. Operational & HR Metadata

  • `Status`: Open, Filled, On Hold.
  • `Approval Status`: Tracks the workflow status of the role.
  • `Location / Region`: The geographic location of the role.
  • `Employment Type`: Full-time, Part-time, Contract, etc.
  • `Salary Band`: The approved compensation range.
  • `Remote Work Policy`: Details on remote work eligibility.
  • `On-call Duties`: Specifies expectations for on-call availability.
  • `Innovation Time`: Policy on dedicated time for innovation.
  • `Activity Log`: An audit trail of changes to the role.
