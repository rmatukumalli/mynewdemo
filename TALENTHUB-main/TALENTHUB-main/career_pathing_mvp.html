Career Pathing - MVP Scope

1. Employee

ID Feature Rationale Depends On Comments Priority
1 ⭐ View career path & possible next roles Employees will not engage without a clear picture of where they can go in the organization. This is the entry point for the entire experience. 8, 16 Career paths cannot be shown without the framework of roles and competencies, and the mapping of feeder/next roles. 4
2 Basic skill gap analysis Gives employees an immediate, tangible reason to update their profiles and start skill development. 5 A skill gap analysis compares an employee’s current skills with those required for their aspirational role. It highlights the specific competencies they need to develop to progress toward that role. 6
3 Recommended learning from LMS integration Connects identified gaps to actionable next steps, ensuring early value. 2, Future Integration Learning recommendations are based on the skill gap analysis and require future LMS integration. 10
4 Track learning progress Keeps employees motivated and provides measurable improvement data from day one. 3 Progress can only be tracked for recommended learning. 13
5 View Role Dictionary Entry Establishes the baseline for growth and ensures alignment with organizational expectations. 8 Role Dictionary provides the basic role information for the MVP. 3
6 Explore next role options and lateral moves Encourages career mobility and retention from the start. 1 This is an extension of viewing the primary career path. 7
7 Basic internal job search with filters Immediate visibility into opportunities drives adoption and positions the platform as the go-to place for internal career moves. Future Integration Job search requires future integration with an ATS. 8

2. Admin (System configuration and management)

ID Feature Rationale Depends On Comments Priority
8 ⭐ Create/Edit Roles in Role Dictionary Allows admins to create a role with title, description, key responsibilities, and associated skills. - This is the existing Role Dictionary functionality. Phase 2 will expand this to full "Role Profiles". 1
9 Career Advancement Policies: Rules for internal progression, including eligibility and approvals (fields in Steps 5-7). Ensures fair internal progression, compliance with HR rules, and clear eligibility/approval processes. 8 These policies are foundational for defining clear career advancement pathways and are informed by data captured in role profiles (see Step 5 and subsequent steps). 5
10 Import roles from CSV Allows for initial setup of role architecture without full integration. - Future integration with ATS, HRIS, and LMS will replace this manual process. 2
16 Enhanced Role Profiles (Phase 2) Expanded role profiles with additional fields including feeder roles and next roles to power career pathing. 8 This is a Phase 2 feature and not part of the MVP. -

3. Hiring Manager

ID Feature Rationale Depends On Comments Priority
11 ⭐ View team skills inventory Enables managers to make immediate use of the platform for workforce planning and targeted development. 5 Team skills are an aggregation of individual employee skills and competencies. 9
12 Approve/decline internal applications Supports the organization’s mobility process and prevents bottlenecks in career movement. 7, 9 Requires the ability for employees to apply for jobs and defined mobility policies. 11
13 Recommend learning or projects to employees Gives managers a proactive role in employee development from the start, building early adoption. 11 Recommendations are based on the team's skills inventory. 12

4. HR Partner (HRBP)

ID Feature Rationale Depends On Comments Priority
14 ⭐ Org-wide mobility analytics (basic dashboards) Allows HR to see immediate trends and adoption, proving ROI early. 1-13 Analytics requires data from all other features to be meaningful. 14
15 Identify skill gaps at department/org level Enables strategic workforce planning and early intervention where critical skills are missing. 14 Org-level skill gap analysis is derived from the mobility analytics. 15
