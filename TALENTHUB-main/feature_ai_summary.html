AI Requirements & Model Concepts

Feature Potential AI Requirement(s) How the AI Model Could Work (Conceptual)
Career Catalyst Personalized learning paths, Intelligent Recommendations, Skill gap analysis Analyzes user profile (skills, goals, activity) and content metadata to recommend relevant learning items, identify skill gaps against career goals, and suggest development steps. Could use collaborative filtering, content-based filtering, or knowledge graph embeddings.
Career Pathing Career pathing and mobility, Skills intelligence Models historical career progression data, skill requirements for roles, and market trends. Uses graph-based algorithms or sequence modeling to suggest viable career trajectories and skills needed for advancement.
Pathfinder Intelligent Recommendations Guides users through career options by matching user profiles (skills, interests, experience) to role requirements and career pathways, potentially using a knowledge graph of roles and skills.
Recommendations Intelligent Recommendations Generates personalized recommendations for jobs, courses, mentors, or projects using techniques like collaborative filtering (user-item interactions) or content-based filtering (item attributes and user profiles).
Employee Skills Dashboard Skill gap analysis, Real-time insights Compares an employee's validated skills against target roles or desired skill sets, highlighting gaps. Provides insights into skill development progress. Could use NLP to suggest skills based on project descriptions.
Employer Skills Dashboard Real-time insights and dashboards, Skills intelligence, Skill gap analysis (org-level) Aggregates workforce skill data to identify organizational strengths, weaknesses, and future needs. Predictive models could forecast skill shortages or surpluses. Uses NLP and machine learning for trend analysis.
Skill Identifier Skill extraction from all content Uses Natural Language Processing (NLP) models (e.g., Named Entity Recognition, text classification) to parse text from resumes, job descriptions, performance reviews, etc., and identify/tag relevant skills.
Skills Graph / Skills Graph Details Skills intelligence AI can help build and maintain the semantic relationships within a skills ontology (e.g., skill similarity, prerequisites, emerging skills) by analyzing large datasets of job postings, academic papers, and learning content.
Skills Intelligence Skill gap analysis, Real-time insights, Predictive analytics A comprehensive AI suite that uses machine learning and NLP to analyze skill trends, benchmark against industry, predict future skill demands, and provide strategic workforce planning insights.
Job Scraper Skill extraction from all content, Job-role-mapping Enhances basic scraping by using NLP to extract structured data like skills, experience levels, and responsibilities from unstructured job descriptions. Can map scraped jobs to an internal job architecture.
Screen Candidates Skill extraction, Candidate-to-job matching, Semantic Search AI parses resumes/CVs to extract skills and experience. Machine learning models rank candidates based on their match to job requirements, potentially using semantic similarity rather than just keyword matching.
Skills Hiring Skill extraction, Job-role-mapping, Skills intelligence Integrates AI across the hiring workflow: identifying skill needs for new roles, sourcing candidates with those skills, assessing skill proficiency, and reducing bias in selection.
Proctored Assessment Behavioral analysis, Anomaly detection (for proctoring); Adaptive testing AI can monitor test-takers for suspicious behavior. Adaptive testing algorithms adjust question difficulty based on user performance, providing a more accurate assessment.
Succession Planning Skills intelligence, Career pathing and mobility, Predictive analytics Identifies high-potential employees by analyzing performance, skills, and learning agility. Predicts leadership potential and suggests development paths for critical roles.
Akara Careers / Akara Job Detail Semantic Search and Discovery Allows users to search for jobs using natural language or by describing desired roles/skills, going beyond simple keyword matching to understand intent and context.
Learning Hub Filters Intelligent Recommendations Suggests relevant learning content (courses, articles, videos) based on a user's skills, career goals, learning history, and the popularity or relevance of content among similar users.

Journey 1: How Employers Post Jobs & Attract Talent

Goal: To demonstrate posting a new "Senior Marketing Manager" role, attracting qualified candidates, and contributing to Akara Airlines' understanding of its hiring skill needs from an employer's perspective.

Steps:

  1. Job Posting Creation & Skill Identification (Features 1 & 4):
    • As a recruiter, I log into the Talent Marketplace platform.
    • I navigate to the "Create Job Posting" section (the Job Posting Destination - Feature 1).
    • As I write the job description for the "Senior Marketing Manager," the Top Skill Identifier (Feature 4) dynamically analyzes the text. It suggests key skills like "Digital Marketing (Expert)," "SEO/SEM (Advanced)," "Content Strategy (Advanced)," and "Team Leadership (Intermediate)."
    • I review these auto-identified skills and their proficiency levels, making minor adjustments as needed.
    • I complete other job details (e.g., salary range, location, reporting structure) and publish the posting.
    • Talk Track Snippet: "As the recruiter, I easily create the job posting, and the system instantly helps me identify and tag the critical skills needed for this role, ensuring clarity for both myself and potential candidates."
  2. Driving Traffic to the Posting (Feature 2):
    • Once published, the platform's Hooks to Drive Traffic (Feature 2) activate. The "Senior Marketing Manager" job posting might be automatically shared on linked professional networks (e.g., LinkedIn), industry-specific job boards, or highlighted to internal employees who might be a good fit or can provide referrals.
    • Talk Track Snippet: "With the click of a button, the job is out there, leveraging various channels to reach the right talent pool quickly."
  3. Configuring Job Scraper for Skills Intelligence (Feature 3 - Akara Airlines Admin Task):
    • Talk Track Snippet: "Now, let's look at a strategic, company-wide feature. Akara Airlines' administrators have configured the Job Scraper . This isn't just about finding candidates for one role; it's about understanding all the skills Akara Airlines is hiring for."
    • An administrator at Akara Airlines (e.g., HR Systems Lead) accesses the Job Scraper configuration panel within the Talent Marketplace.
    • They input the URL of Akara Airlines' own career portal.
    • They specify other major job boards (e.g., LinkedIn, Indeed specific to their industry) where Akara Airlines regularly posts jobs.
    • The Job Scraper is scheduled to run periodically (e.g., daily) to pull job data from these configured sources.
    • Talk Track Snippet: "Admins set this up once, pointing the scraper to their career site and key job boards. It then works automatically in the background."
  4. Synthesizing Hiring Skills & Enhancing Skills Intelligence (Feature 3 & Skills Intelligence Platform):
    • Talk Track Snippet: "The real power of the Job Scraper is in what it does with that data. It extracts skill requirements from all of Akara Airlines' postings."
    • The Job Scraper (Feature 3) collects job descriptions posted by Akara Airlines across the configured sources.
    • AI within the scraper (or linked to the Skills Intelligence platform) parses these descriptions to identify required skills, experience levels, and emerging skill trends *specifically for roles Akara Airlines is hiring for*.
    • This synthesized skill data is fed into Akara Airlines' Skills Intelligence platform (an integral part of the Talent Marketplace, likely enhancing the Employer Skills Dashboard).
    • As a recruiter, I and other HR leaders at Akara Airlines can then access dashboards showing:
      • The most in-demand skills across all their open requisitions.
      • Skill gaps based on current hiring needs versus internal talent.
      • Trends in skills being sought externally by their own company.
    • This intelligence helps Akara Airlines in strategic workforce planning, L&D program development, and refining their overall talent strategy. As the recruiter, I understand that my "Senior Marketing Manager" posting, once scraped, will contribute to this broader organizational insight.
    • Talk Track Snippet: "This feeds directly into their Skills Intelligence. So, as a recruiter, I and the leadership team get a clear, data-driven view of the skills they're actively seeking across the entire organization. This is invaluable for strategic planning, identifying skill gaps, and ensuring their L&D efforts are aligned with real-time hiring needs. My new 'Senior Marketing Manager' role will automatically become part of this analysis."

Journey 2: Candidate Assessment Experience

Goal: To demonstrate how a candidate, let's say Alex Green, successfully completes assigned technical and proctored assessments.

Step 1: Accessing the Assessment Platform

After a candidate like Alex Green applies and is shortlisted for a role, they receive an email invitation with a link to take the required assessments on the Talent Hub.

For demo purposes, I will simulate this journey by navigating to the main platform landing page. From the top navigation bar, I will open the “ATS” dropdown, select “Hiring Manager”, and then go to the “Assessments” tab.

The assessment platform will load. On the initial screen, I'll select the “Candidate” role to proceed, as Alex Green would.

Step 2: Taking a Standard Technical Assessment

Once logged in as a candidate, I (as Alex Green) see a dashboard listing all assigned assessments. For demo purposes, I will select sample assessments like “Senior Frontend Developer Assessment” or “Junior Backend Developer Challenge.”

During the assessment experience, I will encounter:

  • Assessment Overview: This includes the assessment title, company name, and a visible countdown timer.
  • Question Types:
    • Coding Questions – These include a built-in code editor, starter code, and test cases to run and validate solutions.
    • Multiple-Choice Questions – I select an answer from a given list.
    • Text-Based Questions – I type responses in a text area.

I can navigate between questions using “Previous” and “Next” buttons. Answers are auto-saved throughout the process.

Once finished, I click “Submit Assessment.” A confirmation screen appears with a thank-you message.

Step 3: Taking a Proctored Assessment

Back on the candidate dashboard, I also see an option for a Proctored Skill Assessment.

Clicking this option opens a secure, proctored environment in a new window. The interface includes:

  • Clear Instructions on proctoring rules (e.g., camera and microphone permissions, full-screen mode, and anti-cheating measures).
  • Countdown Timer reflecting the allotted duration.
  • Assessment Questions, which may be simplified in the demo version.

If the demo includes simulated proctoring features, I may see alerts for behaviors such as looking away, switching tabs, or background noise—mimicking real-world AI-driven monitoring.

After submission, a completion message confirms that the assessment has been finished. As the candidate, I am instructed to return to the main platform.

Step 4: Reviewing Candidate Submissions (Manager/Admin View)

To demonstrate the review process from a hiring manager’s perspective:

I will navigate back to the “Assessments” tab and select the “Manager” role. The manager dashboard displays submitted assessments, including:

  • Candidate List – View submission status, duration, and overall score.
  • Detailed View – Click on a candidate to see question-level breakdowns:
    • Coding Questions – Submitted code and test case outcomes.
    • MCQs – Selected vs correct answers.
    • Text Responses – Full written answers.

Managers can also access the Analytics section, which shows:

  • Number of submissions
  • Average scores
  • Score distribution ranges
  • Completion rates
  • High-performing candidates

Journey 3: Evaluating Candidates in the ATS

Goal: To demonstrate how I, as a recruiter (e.g., Sarah Miller), evaluate applicants for the "Senior Marketing Manager" role, focusing on skills, and manage them efficiently through the company's Applicant Tracking System (ATS).

Steps:

  1. Assessment Result Review (Features 5 & 6):
    • As the recruiter, I receive a notification that a candidate, let's say Mark, has completed his skills assessment.
    • I log into the Talent Marketplace and view Mark's application. The Evaluation Result (his scores, strengths, and areas for development based on the assessment) is automatically added to his application profile (Feature 6).
    • If needed, I can use the Assessment Builder (Feature 5) to understand the specific questions Mark faced or to customize/create new assessments for future roles based on identified skills.
  2. ATS Integration & Candidate Overview (Features 6 & 7):
    • The Talent Marketplace platform is integrated with our company's ATS (e.g., Greenhouse). Mark's application details, resume, and crucially, his skills assessment results, are automatically synced to Greenhouse (part of ATS Integration - Feature 6).
    • I then log into Greenhouse to manage my candidate pipeline. When I view Mark's profile in Greenhouse, I see the end result in the ATS (Feature 7): a comprehensive candidate view. This includes not just his resume, but also the structured data from the Talent Marketplace – his validated skills, assessment scores, and perhaps even a direct link back to the detailed assessment report. This allows for a more informed and efficient shortlisting process.
